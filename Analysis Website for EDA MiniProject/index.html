About The Project

Understanding Student Commute Patterns

An in-depth analysis of how students travel across Pune, identifying key patterns, pain points, and opportunities for improvement.

Problem Statement

Students in Pune face significant challenges in daily commuting - from long travel times and high costs to unreliable public transport. This analysis aims to quantify these challenges and identify patterns that can inform better transportation planning.

Objectives

  • •Analyze transport mode preferences
  • •Identify peak commute times and patterns
  • •Quantify cost vs. time trade-offs
  • •Map major delay points and bottlenecks

Real-World Relevance

This analysis provides actionable insights for city planners, educational institutions, and transport authorities to improve student commute infrastructure and reduce overall travel burden.

Key Insights

What The Data Reveals

Critical findings and actionable insights from our analysis.

Metro is the Clear Winner

With lowest average cost (₹24) and fastest travel times (~41 min), Metro offers the best value for money and efficiency.

21% of students • prefer Metro

Hinjewadi Traffic Crisis

Hinjewadi Traffic is the most reported bottleneck (17% of delays), followed by University Circle and Waiting for Bus/Metro.

83 delays • reported at Hinjewadi

Cost-Time Trade-off

Cabs cost 10x more than Metro (₹241 vs ₹24) but save only ~5 minutes on average - poor cost efficiency for time saved.

10x cost • for minimal time savings

Morning Peak Dominance

35% of commutes happen during morning peak (8-11 AM), making it the busiest period and a key target for optimization.

254 trips • during morning rush

Satisfaction Paradox

Satisfaction is not strongly correlated with cost or time - subjective factors like comfort and reliability matter significantly.

Average: 2.95/5 • moderate satisfaction

Kothrud: The Commuter Hub

Kothrud contributes the highest number of commuters (15.7%), indicating significant outbound student population from this area.

114 students • from Kothrud area
Conclusion

Summary of Findings

✓ Key Patterns Identified

  • • Metro dominates as the preferred transport mode due to its cost-effectiveness and reliability
  • • Morning peak hours (8-11 AM) see the highest commute volume across all modes
  • • Traffic bottlenecks significantly impact PMPML Bus users more than other commuters
  • • Cost-time efficiency favors public transport over personal vehicles

→ Recommendations

  • • Expand Metro coverage to underserved areas like Hinjewadi and Kharadi
  • • Implement traffic management solutions at identified bottleneck locations
  • • Introduce dedicated bus lanes during peak hours to improve PMPML efficiency
  • • Consider student discount programs for public transport to improve accessibility
